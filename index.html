Virat Kohli-The Run Machine

About Virat Kohli:

  • Virat Kohli was born on 5 November 1988 in Delhi into a Punjabi Hindu family. His father, Prem Nath Kohli, worked as a criminal lawyer, and his mother, Saroj Kohli, served as a housewife. He has an older brother, Vikas, and an older sister, Bhawna.
  • Virat, a 13-year-old, is selected for the Delhi Under-15 team for the Polly Umrigar Trophy in October 2002. By the end of the campaign, he emerges as Delhi's top scorer.
  • After making a mark at the youth level for Delhi, Virat is selected for the India Under-19 team for the first time. During the tour of England, he excels by averaging over 100 in the one-day series.
  • In 2008, Virat led India to triumph in the Under-19 World Cup in Malaysia. He concluded the tournament as one of the top three run-scorers, and his innate leadership abilities garnered widespread attention.
  • In 2009, after a significant wait, Virat rejoined the national team. His patience paid off when he achieved his first ODI century, scoring an impressive 107 runs off 111 balls against Sri Lanka.
  • In 2011, Virat played every match of India's successful World Cup campaign, scoring a century against Bangladesh in the opener. Although his overall performance wasn't stellar, his memorable comment in the final, "Sachin Tendulkar has carried the weight of the nation for 21 years. It's time for us to carry him on our shoulders," has since become iconic in cricket.
  • Virat's exceptional 133* off 86 balls against Sri Lanka in the CB Series propelled India to the final in Australia. His remarkable performance in Hobart, where India chased down 321 in just 37 overs, stands as one of the finest ODI innings by an Indian batsman.
  • In 2013, Virat became the stand-in ODI captain for an injured MS Dhoni during India's West Indies tour. Earlier that year, he was also made captain of his IPL team, Royal Challengers Bangalore.
  • In 2014, Kohli made his debut as Test captain in Australia, filling in for an injured MS Dhoni. He made history by scoring centuries in both innings in Adelaide, marking a memorable start to his leadership journey in Test cricket.
  • In 2018, Virat Kohli reinforced his status as one of the world's premier batsmen by guiding India to their maiden Test series victory in Australia, a historic achievement achieved during his captaincy.
  • n 2024, Virat Kohli, though not captain, left an unforgettable mark in cricket history during the T20 World Cup. His crucial innings of 76 runs in the final against South Africa led India to victory, showcasing his unwavering skill and commitment. This tournament also marked his retirement from T20 internationals
  • Virat Kohli's Test career epitomizes leadership with a record 40 wins in 68 matches as captain, alongside the unprecedented feat of scoring four consecutive double-hundreds in Test series, showcasing unmatched consistency and dedication.
  • Virat Kohli holds the record for the most ODI centuries, with an impressive tally of 50 centuries, marking his exceptional consistency and dominance in one-day international cricket.
  • Virat Kohli holds the record for scoring the most runs in T20Is, amassing a total of 4,042 runs.
  • Virat Kohli has been honored with several prestigious national awards:
    • 2013: Arjuna Award, the second highest sporting honour in India.
    • 2017: Padma Shri, India's fourth highest civilian award.
    • 2018: Major Dhyan Chand Khel Ratna Award, India's highest sporting honour.
